Mr. Trujillo

Name: Roger Trujillo

Age: 47

Gender: Male

Occupation: Elementary school teacher

Technology: iPhone 8, Asus Chromebook 14 (Chrome OS) (work machine), Mac Pro desktop


Attitude:

  • Space is his favorite subject and gets really excited to teach his students about astronomy
  • Loves to find new ways to teach his students
  • Overworked but also gets much joy from teaching his students

Needs/Goals/Features:

  • Wants an accessible tool to share with his students and encourage interest in astronomy
  • Needs simple interface that doesn’t require too much tech experience

Frustrations:

  • Lack of decent/accessible app to educate students with factoids and additional info
  • Simple interface for students

Goals:

  • He wants to be able to show information to his students in classroom and web conferencing settings.

User Story:

As a user, I need an easy resource that students can take advantage of on their own with little instruction needed outside the classroom.


Use Case:

Title:Look up a particular planet to show the class

Description:He wants to look up Mars and show it to his class

Name Of User & Role:Roger Trujillo, teacher

Usage Preconditions:Must navigate to website

Usage Postconditions:Mars information is displayed


Use Interaction Flow:

  • Roger enters URL into web browser and is already logged in with cookies
  • Server returns website main page
  • Roger selects Planets > Mars in the navbar
  • Server returns Planets page at #mars location
  • Roger clicks ‘Like’ on Mars card
  • Server completes request to add ‘Like’ to Mars
  • Roger sees that the ‘Like’ button (star shape) is now filled in
  • Roger clicks on Profile tab in the navbar
  • The server returns his profile page
  • Roger sees that Mars is included in his list of ‘Likes’
